Experience

Birdflop | CEO

Sept. 2017 - Present

I started a Minecraft server back in 2017, and it got so popular that my host's hardware specifications could not keep up and it started to lag. I switched from host to host until I eventually decided that I needed my own dedicated machine. To save costs, I decided to colocate a machine rather than rent. I spent days researching, building, and shipping my server to New York. Other server owners who used to play on my server came to me asking to do the same for their servers. I noticed that my machine still had the resources to host a couple more servers so I added them to my machine. Word spread about my hosting services and more server owners started to contact me for hosting, I bought additional machines. This turned into Birdflop, and I now have seven nodes hosted across three countries around the world.
